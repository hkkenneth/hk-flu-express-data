FLU EXPRESS
Flu Express is a weekly report produced by the Respiratory Disease Office of the Centre for Health Protection. It monitors
and summarizes the latest local and global influenza activities.

Local Situation of Influenza Activity (as of Apr 26, 2012)
Reporting period: Apr 15– 21, 2012 (Week 16) 






The latest surveillance data showed that the overall influenza activity still 
remained at high level. 
The Centre for Health Protection has collaborated with the Hospital Authority and 
private hospitals to monitor influenza associated intensive care unit (ICU) 
admissions or deaths (aged 18 years or above) since Jan 13, 2012. As of Apr 25, 
there were 124 cases of influenza associated ICU admissions or deaths, in which 
72 of them were fatal. 
As Hong Kong is still in winter influenza season, target groups are advised to make 
use of the last opportunity to receive influenza vaccinations.

Enhanced Surveillance for Severe Seasonal Influenza (Aged 18 years or above) 


From Apr 18, 2012, 12:00 noon to Apr 25, 2012, 12:00 noon, 5 cases of influenza 
associated ICU admissions or deaths were recorded, in which 2 of them were fatal. In 
addition, one previously reported case who required ICU admission passed away during 
this reporting period.

Surveillance of severe paediatric influenza‐associated complication/death (Aged 
above one month and below 18 years) 


In week 16, there were no new reports of severe paediatric influenza‐associated 
complication/death. In the first 4 days of week 17 (Apr 22 to 25, 2012), one case of severe 
paediatric influenza‐associated complication involving a boy aged 7 years old who had 
developed severe tracheitis was reported. He had been discharged. His nasopharyngeal 
aspirate specimen was tested positive for influenza A. 
Note: The data reported are provisional figures and subject to further revision.

Surveillance of Tamiflu resistant influenza A(H1N1)2009 virus infection 


In week 16 and the first 4 days of week 17 (Apr 22 to 25, 2012), there were no new 
reports of oseltamivir (Tamiflu) resistant influenza A(H1N1)2009 virus infection. There are 
totally 18 reports of resistance to Tamiflu in strains of influenza A(H1N1)2009 virus 
detected in Hong Kong since 2009. 

Global Situation of Influenza Activity


In the United States (week ending Apr 14, 2012), influenza activity was elevated in some 
areas, but declined nationally and in most regions. 



In Canada (week ending Apr 14, 2012), the peak of activity for the 2011‐2012 influenza 
season has passed as most indicators of influenza activity continued to decline. 



In the United Kingdom (week ending Apr 15, 2012), the weekly influenza consultation 
rates in England, Wales, Scotland and Northern Ireland remained low. 



In Europe (as of Apr 15 2012), ILI consultation rate continued to decrease, and most of the 
countries were at low influenza intensity. 



In Singapore (week ending Apr 21, 2012), the number of consultations for acute 
respiratory infections was above the warning level.
