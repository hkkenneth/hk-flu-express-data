FLU EXPRESS
Flu Express is a weekly report produced by the Respiratory Disease Office of the Centre for Health Protection. It monitors
and summarizes the latest local and global influenza activities.

Local Situation of Influenza Activity (as of Feb 2, 2012)
Reporting period: Jan 22 – 28, 2012 (Week 4) 






The latest surveillance data showed that the overall influenza activity has 
continued to increase. 
The Centre for Health Protection has collaborated with the Hospital Authority and 
private hospitals to monitor influenza associated intensive care unit (ICU) 
admissions or deaths (aged 18 years or above) since Jan 13, 2012. As of Feb 1, 
there were 12 cases of influenza associated ICU admissions or deaths, in which 7 
of them were fatal. 
The local influenza activity is expected to increase further during the next few 
weeks. Target groups are advised to receive influenza vaccinations as soon as 
possible to protect their health.

Surveillance of severe paediatric influenza‐associated complication/death (Aged 
above one month and below 18 years) 


In week 4, one case of severe paediatric influenza‐associated complication involving a 
9‐year‐old boy who had developed myocarditis was reported. He had been discharged. In 
the first 4 days of week 5 (Jan 29 to Feb 1, 2012), one case of severe paediatric 
influenza‐associated complication involving a 6‐year‐old girl who had developed 
myocarditis was reported. She is now in stable condition. Both cases had their 
nasopharyngeal aspirate specimens tested positive for influenza B. 
Note: The data reported are provisional figures and subject to further revision.

Surveillance of Tamiflu resistant influenza A(H1N1)2009 virus infection 


In week 4 and the first 4 days of week 5 (Jan 29 to Feb 1, 2012), there were no new 
reports of oseltamivir (Tamiflu) resistant influenza A(H1N1)2009 virus infection. There are 
totally 16 reports of resistance to Tamiflu in strains of influenza A(H1N1)2009 virus 
detected in Hong Kong since 2009. 

Global Situation of Influenza Activity


In the United States (week ending Jan 21, 2012), influenza activity remained relatively 
low. 



In the United Kingdom (week ending Jan 22, 2012), influenza activity remained low in 
England, Wales, Scotland and Northern Ireland. 



In Europe (as of Jan 22, 2012), influenza activity has increased consistently over the past 
several weeks, but influenza detections remained low compared to the same period of 
the 2010/2011 season. 



In Singapore (week ending Jan 28, 2012), the number of consultations for acute 
respiratory infections was below the warning level.
